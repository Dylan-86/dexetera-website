Security & Transparency First

Trust must be verified. At Dexetera, our core architecture ensures you never have to trust us—you only need to trust the code.

Absolute Self-Custody

Dexetera does not hold your USDC. Our smart contracts operate strictly on a non-custodial basis. You control your private keys, and only cryptographic signatures from your wallet can authorize trades or withdrawals.

On-Chain Auditability

Every matching event, funding rate update, liquidation, and order execution is immutably recorded on the Hyperliquid blockchain. The entire state of the exchange can be publicly audited in real-time by anyone.

Smart Contract Bound

The mechanics of Dexetera are hardcoded into highly optimized, open-source smart contracts. These contracts are continuously peer-reviewed and subjected to rigorous formal verification to prevent complex attack vectors.

Infrastructure Integrity

The Hyperliquid Advantage

By building exclusively on Hyperliquid, we eliminate the bridging risks and latency attacks common on generalized chains. Hyperliquid is a specialized L1 engineered specifically for order book exchanges. Consensus itself is optimized for secure trade matching, meaning Dexetera benefits from native, protocol-level security rather than vulnerable Layer-2 abstractions.

Oracle-less Data Verifiability

Since Dexetera enables trading "anything measurable," we operate on a fundamentally Oracle-less architecture. We don't rely on centralized or third-party oracle networks. Instead, the outcome of any market is verified at expiration using an external data source URL—defined transparently by the user who created the market. This source is rigorously validated by an AI mechanism to ensure settlement accuracy.

Liquidity Driven by Longs

Unlike traditional AMMs or Oracle-dependent protocols, the liquidity of a Dexetera contract is directly provided and backed by the "long" positions themselves. This pure peer-to-peer structure guarantees that every contract is fully collateralized by real market demand, eliminating systemic liquidation cascades.
